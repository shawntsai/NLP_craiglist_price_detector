2009 BMW F800GS w/10,951 miles. Base price new was $10,520 and I paid $15,912 for it new from Bob's BMW dealership in Maryland with the BMW options listed below. I added lots of extras immediately after purchase, placing the bike around $17K new.

The Kelly Blue Book base price is $6,275 not including the BMW options and aftermarket extras, with mileage between 27K and 40K. My mileage is much, much lower, and it has almost every BMW option and aftermarket extras imaginable.

BMW options include: BMW Comfort Package (heated grips and ABS), BMW clear turn signals, BMW center stand, BMW Zumo satellite navigation system (turn by turn instructions, road maps, compass, waypoints, etc. The unit connects to your computer for trip planning, software updates, etc.).

Extras include: One Givi top and two Givi side luggage cases (an Italian brand, they include Givi's integrated brake lighting system), ZTechnik side mirror extenders (kicks the mirrors out to give you a better view of what's going on behind you), ZTechnik V-Stream tall windscreen (cuts wind noise, reduces riding fatigue, and provides extra protection from road debris), Rox Speed FX handle bar riser/extender (for better ergonomics and control), AltRider full-wrap engine bars (protects engine and fairing from oops forgot to put the kick stand down moments and oh-by-the-way it makes the bike look way cooler), off-road skid plate (protects bottom of engine from rocks and debris), hand protectors (protects hands from rocks and debris), Wunderlich adjustable folding gear shift lever (reduces boot wear to a minimum and makes shifting a breeze), rear brake light strobe flasher (for safety), and an extra large off-road kickstand platform.

I had the Givi luggage cases and brake light strobe flasher professionally hard wired at Bob's BMW Motorcycles. Hit the brakes and the back of the bike lights up like a carnival. I also applied reflective tape along the sides of the cases for added reflection at night.

There's light rust around the screws along the brake rotors and along some screws here and there which can be brushed off with a wire brush. There's light wear and light scratches on the fairing and around the bike overall, but no major damage or dents. The sun caught the windscreen just right to melt the odometer housing slightly, but the gauges still work great. A new F800GS decal is on its way. A few of the LED lights on the rear strobe flashers need to be replaced and one Givi brake light connector needs to be replaced (it's currently secured with electrical tape).

The bike was professionally serviced, annually, at Bob's BMW Motorcycles in Maryland and serviced last week at BMW Motorcycles in Long Beach, CA. Long Beach BMW just adjusted and lubed the chain as well. All service stamps are in the original owner's manual. I have all keys and spare keys and the original BMW short-rise sport windscreen is included.

It looks right out of Terminator, right? I still get compliments all of the time. It's an incredibly smooth machine. I love it but I have to let it go.

All sales final. Cashier's check only. I am the original owner and the Maryland title is clear. The bike is registered in California and I have temporary tags in California. This is baby mileage for a BMW. You'll enjoy this machine for years to come.
